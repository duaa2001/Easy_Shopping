BIPOC

If you're looking for small or BIPOC (Black Indigenous Person of Color) owned businesses you've come to the right place. Here are links to get you started!

Beauty Bakerie

Beauty Bakerie was founded by Cashmere Nicole in 2011 while battling breast cancer. She began working on Beauty Bakerie after finishing college and nursing school while also juggling being a single mom. Nicole went viral after Beyonce decided to feature Nicole's story on Queen Bey's site in October of 2014.

LaSette

Shiara Robinson founded LaSette in 2017. Robinson wanted women to feel empowered in the lingerie she created with comfort being the top priority. "Transitional lingerie designed for day to night lifestyles -- to be worn as seen fit...LaSette, was designed with you in mind, to create your own storyline for lingerie. Wear to work for back to back meetings, mix, match and layer with your favorite pieces to create the perfect “meeting up for drinks” look, drop it like it’s hot at the club, or wear around the house. Designed for day to night lifestyles, inspired by my life in New York City over the past 6 years, and the sidewalk fashionista’s making any and everything work!" (LaSette)

The Tiny Tassel

"Mimi, a College Of Charleston 2014 Graduate, left life in the haberdashery world behind and founded The Tiny Tassel during the summer of 2015. Designing and creating each tassel by hand with hopes of bringing a little joy to life has led her to offer a full line of handmade tassel jewelry, garments (made by her Mom!) & even party decor." Uoma Beauty

"UOMA - pronounced OMA (uh-mah) means beautiful and we believe that beauty starts the moment you decide to be yourself. Founded by Nigerian born, LA & London based former beauty executive Sharon Chuter, the UOMA Beauty range is rebellious, innovative and created for all. At UOMA we are an empowered tribe. Sotela

"An inclusive clothing brand and community that celebrates humans in all their forms. Sotela's pieces are forgiving. Our sizing focuses on fit instead of labeling; we never use the words small, medium, or large. We champion body appreciation and celebrate strength and resilience through our For All Community. We use eco-friendly fabrics that have minimal environmental impacts such as tencel, modal, and linen. The versatility of our pieces allow for prolonged use and all of our packaging, stationery, and marketing materials are printed on recycled paper. We hand-make every piece from start to finish in our California studio." (Sotela)
